Game Sudoku

Gameplay: Grid: The Sudoku grid is composed of 9 rows, 9 columns, and 9 3x3 subgrids, making a total of 81 cells. Digits: Use the digits 1 through 9 to fill the grid. Each digit can only appear once in each row, column, and 3x3 subgrid. Starting Grid: At the beginning of the game, some cells will already have numbers (given as clues), and your task is to fill in the rest. Rules for Placement: Each row must contain all digits from 1 to 9 without repetition. Each column must contain all digits from 1 to 9 without repetition. Each 3x3 subgrid (region) must contain all digits from 1 to 9 without repetition. Strategy: Sudoku requires logical thinking and deduction to fill in the grid correctly. Here are some common strategies: Elimination: Use the numbers already placed on the board to eliminate possibilities for other cells in the same row, column, or subgrid. Candidates: Identify possible numbers (candidates) for each empty cell based on the numbers already present in its row, column, and subgrid. Single Possibility: Sometimes a cell can only logically contain one possible number due to the constraints of the puzzle. Solving: Continue filling in numbers one by one, following the rules and using deduction until the entire grid is filled correctly. Completion: The puzzle is solved when all cells are filled correctly according to the rules of Sudoku.
