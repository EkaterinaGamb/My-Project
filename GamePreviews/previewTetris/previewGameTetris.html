Game Tetris

Gameplay: Blocks (Tetrominoes): Tetris is played on a vertical grid-based playing field. Blocks, known as tetrominoes, consisting of four square blocks each, fall from the top of the screen. Movement: You can move the falling tetrominoes left or right using the left and right arrow keys (or similar controls). You can also rotate the tetrominoes clockwise or counterclockwise using the up arrow key or another designated key. Objective: The main objective is to manipulate the falling tetrominoes to create complete horizontal lines (rows) of blocks without gaps. Clearing Lines: When a horizontal line of blocks is completely filled without any gaps, it is cleared from the playing field. Scoring: Points are awarded for each line cleared. More points are awarded for clearing multiple lines simultaneously (known as a "Tetris"). Speed: As you progress through the game, the tetrominoes fall faster, increasing the difficulty and pace of the game. Game Over Conditions: The game ends when the stack of blocks reaches the top of the playing field, making it impossible to place new tetrominoes. Some versions of Tetris may also end if you exceed a certain number of misplaced blocks.
