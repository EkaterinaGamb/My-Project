Game Snake

Gameplay: Snake: You control a snake represented by a line or series of connected blocks on a grid-based playing field. Movement: The snake moves continuously in a straight line in the direction it is facing. You can control the direction of the snake's movement using arrow keys (or other controls depending on the device). Eating Food: Randomly placed on the game board is food, often represented by a small dot or icon. The snake must navigate to and consume this food to grow longer. Growing Length: Each time the snake eats food, it grows longer by one segment or block. Game Over Conditions: Collision with Itself: If the snake's head collides with any part of its own body, the game ends. Collision with Walls: If the snake moves into the boundaries of the game area or into any obstacles (if present), the game ends. Scoring: Points are typically awarded for each piece of food eaten. The score increases as the snake gets longer and eats more food. Game Progression: As the snake grows longer, the game becomes more challenging because it occupies more space on the board, making it easier to collide with itself or obstacles.
